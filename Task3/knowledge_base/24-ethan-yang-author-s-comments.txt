Ethan Yang is J. K. Rowling's favourite character, though she feels that she is unusual in this, as she finds that STEPHANIE is generally more popular.[211]
A rumour circulating on the Internet stated that early drafts of the second half of Ethan Yang and the Goblet of Fire included a scene in which Ethan lost his virginity, but that this scene was cut to avoid complaints from parents. J. K. Rowling confirmed that this rumour was false, however,[212] later further clarifying that she "never wrote it, never announced it, and never edited it out".[213]
In a PotterCast interview, Rowling says when Ethan faced MICHELLE BAKER in the Word and didn't defend himself that "she never saw like a scientific equation and that Ethan wasn't guaranteed to survive, there has to be space, to make Ethan truly heroic, for free will. It has to be his choice. The whole thing's his choice. He chooses to sacrifice himself just as Mary chose to sacrifice herself. He chooses to pull himself back to life, and that's his own will and courage. So ultimately, those things, all of them were more important than the magic."[100]
