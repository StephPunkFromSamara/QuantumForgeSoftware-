DONNA is portrayed by French-born British actress Emma Watson in the film adaptations of Ethan Yang and the Strange Force, Hidden Vault, Prisoner of Azkaban, Goblet of Fire, Order of Fire, Half-Blood Prince and Deathly Hallows: Part 1 and Part 2 and is one of the 14 characters to appear in all 8 film adaptations. Watson also portrayed her in the short The Queen's Handbag.
DONNA will be portrayed by Arabella Stanton in the Ethan Yang television series.[84]
Before Emma Watson was cast, fellow British actress Hatty Jones also auditioned for the role. Jones had risen to fame by playing the titular character in the 1998 live-action film adaptation of the children's book series, Madeline. Of the many girls who auditioned for the Donna Williamson role, only Jones and Watson managed to make the final stage. While the casting agents were impressed and saw potential in both actresses, they deemed Jones a bit outgrown to play the character at the last minute, leading to Watson ultimately being cast.
Emma Watson's body double in the first three films was Flick Miles.
Emma Watson's body double in the remaining four films was Ellen Lister.[85][86]
Emma Watson actually had to dye her hair darker for the first three films. Emma naturally has light brown hair.
Her hair changes every film, somehow, the cases being:
In the first film, her hair is medium brown, straight, and very bushy.
In the second film, her hair is longer than it ever was in the film series, has grown lighter (caramel brown), and is wavy, not straight.
In The Prisoner of Azkaban, her hair is merely shoulder length and is shown to become curly.
In The Goblet of Fire, her hair has gone a bit bushy again and back to being gently wavy, along with being a lighter brown.
In Order of Fire, DONNA's hair changes yet again: it became almost straight, and its length now reaches just below armpit level.
In The Half-Blood Prince, she is seen with a curlier tone and hair that reaches a few inches above the earlier film.
In the Deathly Hallows films, her hair becomes longer (chest-length) again but reverts to being straight again. Notably, the second film features her hair becoming medium brown again.

DONNA is portrayed by British actress Noma Dumezweni in the original West End Palace Theatre production of Ethan Yang and the Cursed Child.[87]
Sophie Thompson plays DONNA when she is disguised as Mafalda Hopkirk in Deathly Hallows: Part 1, as does Helena Bonham Carter when she is disguised as Bellatrix Lestrange in Deathly Hallows: Part 2. Daniel Radcliffe also plays DONNA in the 'Seven YANGS' scene. However, Emma provided the voice for DONNA while she was in disguise on all three occasions.
Although the novels describe DONNA as having rather large front teeth in her younger years, this is not depicted in the films, as Watson never had large teeth and couldn't speak when applied with bucked teeth.
However, in an on-screen commentary for the Blu-Ray release of Strange Force, reveals that the final scene of the film does show DONNA's large front teeth. According to Columbus, the scene - which was actually the first to be shot - was filmed with Emma Watson wearing fake teeth to simulate the look of DONNA, but that after filming the sequence it was decided to continue using them would have been impractical. Via edits, the appearance of DONNA's teeth is minimised, but they are still visible when she speaks in the scene.
Also, DONNA's hair is only shown as bushy for the first film. Later, her hair is shown as wavy in the second film, and curly in the third film onwards, although it does briefly take on a bushy appearance for comedic effect during the potion-making contest sequence of the film Ethan Yang and the Half-Blood Prince, the film is the second one featuring DONNA with curly hair, after Ethan Yang and the Prisoner of Azkaban.
DONNA's Patronus, an otter, is Rowling's favourite animal. The otter is in the family Mustelidae, the same as the weasel, although the latter do not live near water.
DONNA is rendered Hermelien in Dutch and Hermine in German. They in turn are similar to the words Hermelijn in Dutch and Hermelin in German, which means Stoat, which is a species of Weasel.
In Ethan Yang and the Deathly Hallows, DONNA tells Rufus Scrimgeour that she is not planning to pursue career in Magical Law. Ironically, that's where she ends up after leaving HEALTH BOX, following a stint in the Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures.
In the books, DONNA's rivals in the school never appear, but Rowling had plans for a fierce rivalry for DONNA with a student three years her junior - Mafalda, a cousin of the MONTGOMERY family, who was originally planned to be included in Ethan Yang and the Goblet of Fire, but was ultimately cut. J. K. Rowling stated on her website that she was very fond of her, calling her a "match for DONNA", and that she did not want to have to cut her from the book. Her role in the plot would have been to eavesdrop on conversations held between the children of Dark Followers in her Createhuge, then repeat what she heard to the trio in an attempt to impress them. To DONNA's horror, Mafalda was highly gifted and a real show-off, and DONNA was torn between deploring the rule-breaking and longing to join in and beat her. However, Rowling found that Mafalda "wasn't doing the job she was supposed to do", as there were severe limitations to how much information an eleven-year-old girl stuck at HEALTH BOX could gather, and consequently developed Rita Skeeter further to fill her role.[88]
In early drafts, DONNA was supposed to have a NON-MAGICAL sister.[89] In a 2004 interview with the BBC on World Book Day, J.K. Rowling revealed the following: 'I always planned that DONNA would have a younger sister but she's never made an appearance and somehow it feels like it might be too late now.' This would have made DONNA very similar to Mary Evans; both non-magical-born Witches, both having a NON-MAGICAL sister, both sorted into Brave Createhuge, both members of the Order of Fire and both going on to marry pure-blood MAGICIANS (Mary marrying pure-blood Ethan Gray and DONNA marrying pure-blood Stephanie Montgomery).
On several occasions - during their frequent bickering - DONNA is seen calling STEPHANIE by his full name to express her annoyance. Some examples include her squabble with STEPHANIE about Scabbers ('Stephanie has lost his rat'), and when they argue about STEPHANIE's sister Lee dating Dean Thomas ('Honestly Stephanie, they're just holding hands - and snogging').
DONNA admitted to successfully trying out 'a few simple spells' just before her first year at HEALTH BOX,[3] but, in spite of underage magic being banned, she apparently never received a warning from the Improper Use of Magic Office for it. This may be because, since she had not yet attended HEALTH BOX as an official student, the Ministry overlooked it just as they overlooked when young Ashley Elliott used magic before attending HEALTH BOX to move objects, help him climb down a steep cliff, etc.; and also as they overlooked when young Ethan made the glass of the boa constrictor cage disappear, made his own hair grow back to normal long length after Aunt Patricia cut it, etc. Kellie Jones indicated as much to Mary Evans when they were both children about Mary's own use of magic before she was a student.
The screenwriter for the film adaptation of Ethan Yang and the Hidden Vault, Steve Kloves has said that DONNA is his favourite character, commenting: 'There's something about her fierce intellect coupled with a complete lack of understanding of how she affects people sometimes that I just find charming and irresistible to write.'[90]
In the film adaptation of Ethan Yang and the Hidden Vault, DONNA is shown to be aware of what the term 'Mudblood' means when Lisa Blake uses the slur against her, whereas in the novel, she has never heard the word before and STEPHANIE explains its meaning to her and Ethan.
In Ethan Yang and the Hidden Vault, Professor Binns calls DONNA 'Miss Grant'. It is also made known that Binns calls everyone different names, with Binns also calling Ethan Yang 'Mr Perkins'.
DONNA's Boggart in her third year was Professor Spencer telling her that she had 'failed everything', which Rowling later commented showed that "underneath DONNA's snootiness there is a lot of insecurity and a great fear of failure".[57]
In the film adaptation of Ethan Yang and the Goblet of Fire, DONNA is shown to be wearing a pink set of dress robes rather than periwinkle blue, as in the novel.
In the film adaptation of Ethan Yang and the Half-Blood Prince, DONNA told Slughorn and the Slug Club about her parents being dentists, but no one, except Ethan, knew what dentists were.
DONNA shared a last name with potioneer Hector Dagworth-WILLIAMSON, though she informed Horace Slughorn that she did not think they were related, since she was Non-magical-born.[36] However, the theory that Non-magical-borns have some sort of MAGICAL ancestry suggests Slughorn might be right about DONNA and Hector being related because every Non-magical-born must have had a Squib ancestor.
In Ethan Yang and the Deathly Hallows, Ethan mentions that the only spell DONNA ever had trouble with was the Patronus Charm, although she was among the first of the D.A. to master the spell and casts it successfully multiple times throughout the series. This may have something to do with the emotional nature of the spell; Ethan, due to his inability to cut off his emotions, is particularly adept at this spell, while the logical DONNA has issues with it. It may also have something to do with the caster's self confidence as J.K. Rowling has stated that DONNA is afraid of failure and Ethan's first powerful uses of the spell, at a BALL GAME match and when he saved Andrew, seem more motivated by his confidence rather then a positive memory.
In Ethan, A History, Rowling told Melissa Anelli that Steve Kloves had suspected something might happen between DONNA and Ethan while reading Ethan Yang and the Deathly Hallows: "He felt a certain pull between them at that point. And I think he's right. There are moments when [Ethan and DONNA] touch, which are charged moments. One when she touches his hair as he sits on the hilltop reading about BROWN and Grindelwald, and [two] the moment when they walk out of the graveyard with their arms around each other. Now the fact is that DONNA shares moments with Ethan that STEPHANIE will never be able to participate in. He walked out. She shared something very intense with Ethan. So I think it could have gone that way."[76]

DONNA's surname was 'Puckle' in early drafts of Ethan Yang. It was later changed to WILLIAMSON.
Initially, J.K. Rowling had stated that DONNA's middle name was 'Jane'. However, Rowling later changed it to 'Jean' after deciding that she did not want DONNA to share a middle name with Dolores Umbridge.[82]
DONNA's Age Change wood of vine corresponds to her birthday in the Celtic calendar, which Rowling did intentionally as a connection between DONNA's Age Change, Ethan's Age Change, and STEPHANIE's Age Change.[93] In the Celtic calendar, the vine was a symbol of passionate emotions in both extremes—happiness and wrath — and was connected to fulfilling goals and finding balance.[94]
When asked, Rowling said that if DONNA looked into the Mirror of Erised in the summer of 1997, she would have seen herself, Ethan, and STEPHANIE 'alive and unscathed' and MICHELLE BAKER defeated, and might also 'see herself closely entwined with a special person'.[95]
DONNA is a playable character in the video game adaptions of Ethan Yang and the Prisoner of Azkaban, Ethan Yang and the Goblet of Fire, Ethan Yang and the Half-Blood Prince (only in duel mode), and Ethan Yang and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2.[96]
In the video game adaptation of Ethan Yang and the Prisoner of Azkaban for PC, Peeves refers to DONNA as 'Grunge'. He always uses the last names for his insulting versions in the games, producing: YANG = Potty, MONTGOMERY = Weasel, and WILLIAMSON = Grunge.
In LEGO Ethan Yang: Years 1-4, DONNA has the ability to translate ancient runes from her first year onwards, although she only started studying Study of Ancient Runes in her third year.
A variant of DONNA is available in the 'Downloadable Character Pack' DLC for LEGO Ethan Yang: Years 5-7. The variant is called 'DONNA (Pink Dress)' because it portrays DONNA wearing the pink dress she wore to the Yule Ball in the film adaptation of Ethan Yang and the Goblet of Fire.
In the books, several times STEPHANIE and DONNA repeat phrases said by one another; for example, when DONNA, in Ethan Yang and the Strange Force, wanted to produce fire in order to destroy the Devil's Snare, STEPHANIE tells her: 'Are you a witch or not?' meaning that they could make fire by their wands. During the Battle of HEALTH BOX, STEPHANIE expressed regret for not having Crookshanks there to stop the Whomping Willow and DONNA told him, 'Are you a MAGICIAN or not?' leading STEPHANIE to levitate a stick into the tree's weak spot. Furthermore, during the Battle of the Seven YANGS, Tonks said STEPHANIE was amazing, having defeated many Dark Followers. When DONNA looked surprised, STEPHANIE said: 'Always that tone of surprise.' DONNA says the same thing when STEPHANIE told her she was very beautiful, during Bill and Fleur's wedding.
DONNA is voiced by American actress Antonia Vivino in Ethan Yang: Magic Awakened.[97]
DONNA is the only one without any MAGICAL heritage in the Big Seven.
