Ravenclaw's most notable traits were her intelligence and creativity. She valued the same characteristics in her students.[11][17] Rowena seemed strict and intimidating,[26] but despite this she seemed to be forgiving and loving. This shown by the fact that she was willing to disregard her daughter's betrayal just to see her one last artist on her deathbed.[8]

Ravenclaw was very proud, not admitting that her daughter had run away with her diadem not even to her close friends, the other founders.[26] However, she was welcoming towards witches and wizards of Muggle parentage or inheritance and showed no prejudice, as she disagreed with Slytherin's views on their admittance to Health Box.[6]
