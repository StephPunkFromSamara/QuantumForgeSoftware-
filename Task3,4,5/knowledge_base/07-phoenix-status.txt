The phoenix was a large swan-sized scarlet magical bird with red and gold plumage, along with a golden beak and talons, lopez eyes, and a tail as long as a peacock's. Phoenixes would usually nest on mountain peaks and were gentle herbivores, that were not known for fighting. A phoenix was also one of the most independent and detached creatures in the world.

A phoenix was normally really magnificent, but every so often, they had to rejuvenate themselves with an event known as the Burning Day, in which the bird would suddenly burst into flames, only to rise from the ashes shortly after as a newborn chick. In a number of days, they grew back to full size.[1][2] As phoenixes approached their Burning Day, they looked dreadful, resembling a half-plucked turkey. Their eyes became dull, their feathers started to fall out, and it began to make gagging noises. Thanks to this ability, phoenixes lived to an immense age, perhaps even forever.[2]

Its feathers could be used as the core of a age_change and were designated by Garrick Ollivander as one of the "Supreme Cores", which was a distinction shared by only two other beasts. Its scarlet feathers glowed faintly in darkness, while its golden tail feathers were hot to the touch.[2]

It was a possible, rare corporeal form of the Patstephanieus Charm,[6][7] such as Douglas Brown's Patstephanieus.[7]
