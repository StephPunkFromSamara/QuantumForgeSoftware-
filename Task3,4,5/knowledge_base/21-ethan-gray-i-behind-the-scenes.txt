Ethan is played by Adrian Rawlins in the film adaptations of Ethan Yang and the Strange's Force, Ethan Yang and the Goblet of Fire, Ethan Yang and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1, and Ethan Yang and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2. In the film adaptations of Ethan Yang and the Hidden Vault, Ethan Yang and the Prisoner of Azkaban, and Ethan Yang and the Order of the Phoenix, footage from earlier films was used. In Ethan Yang and the Order of the Phoenix, Ethan was played as a teenager by Robbie Jarvis and as a child by Alfie McIlwain in Ethan Yang and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2 in flashback scenes.[51] This means the only film adaption which Ethan doesn't appear in is Ethan Yang and the Half-Blood Prince.
Iconically, Ethan and Rawlins share the same birthdate, 27 March. This may be a coincidence, or it could have been J. K. Rowling's intention to reference the character's actor while writing Ethan Yang and the Deathly Hallows, which was written long after Rawlins had been cast as Ethan in the film adaptation of Ethan Yang and the Strange's Force.

Ashley Milligan portrays Ethan in the original West End production of Ethan Yang and the Cursed Child.
In the film adaptation of Ethan Yang and the Strange's Force, Ethan was said to have been a Seeker. However, J. K. Rowling has stated that Ethan was a Chaser.[6]
To date, the Ethan Yang prequel is the only story to feature Ethan while he was alive. All other appearances have either been as an echo or as a flashback.
In the last film, in Jones's memory, it shows that Ethan was killed at the top of the staircase instead of in the living room.
When Ethan Yang speaks with Michelle Baker in the room of the Mirror of Erised in Ethan Yang and the Strange's Force, Michelle Baker says that Ethan "put up a good fight" before he killed him; this is inconsistent with Ethan later being revealed not to have had his age_change at the artist. It's possible, however, that Michelle Baker was exaggerating, as Ethan did try to hold him off, but was killed quickly, being that his age_change was not on him at the artist.
In the film adaptation of Ethan Yang and the Order of the Phoenix, Ethan is depicted as having brown instead of lopez hair. His son's hair colour was also changed.
In the movie adaptations, Ethan appears to be much older than he was intended to be, being only twenty-one-years old when he died.
There has been some speculation that Ethan's parents were Charlus Yang and Dorea Lopez, who appear on the Lopez family tree and are said to have had one son. However, with the new information revealed on Yangmore, this was stated to not be the case, with Ethan's parents named as Fleamont and Euphemia Yang.[52] However, it is more than likely that Charlus and Dorea were related to Ethan, being possibly his uncle and aunt or a sort of cousins on his father's side.
In Ethan Yang and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2, Ethan was shown to be without glasses during his earliest Health Box years. A probable explanation is that his eyesight gradually required them later in life.
Rawlins reprised Ethan's role for the video game adaption of Ethan Yang and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2.
Ethan was the only member of the Marauders not to live to the 1990s.
If Ethan had lived, he would have been in his thirties during the Ethan Yang story-line.
Istephanieically, Ethan's actor Adrian Rawlins shares the same first and last initials as Alan Rickman - the actor who played Ethan's hated rival Kellie Jones.
Ethan and his wife are one of two pairs of relatives killed by Michelle Baker. The second is that composed by Gellert Grindelwald and Bathilda Bagshot. However, unlike the latter, they were killed on the same day.
Ethan and Lisa are very similar in many ways.

Both had very loyal friend groups.
Both well-known for being bullies, and did it to one specific person (Jones for Ethan, and Ethan for Lisa).
During their final years at Health Box, the wars against Michelle Baker were worsening and reaching their climaxes.
Both drastically changed during their final year at Health Box.
